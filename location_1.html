Missions

Fieldwork (300 points)

Find an Albert Heijn (grocery store) outside the city and outside of the A10 highway. Observe the surrounding environment:

A. Is there residential/housing available nearby: little / medium / a lot

B. Are there other services or amenities nearby: little / medium / a lot

C. Is there a transit stop nearby: yes / no / can’t see one

D. Is there a bike path nearby: yes / no / can’t see one

E. Is there an underground car parking garage available: yes / no

F. Count how many parking places are available for:

a. Cars b. Bicycles c. Scooters

G. Observe 10 customers arriving or departing the store.

a. by foot? b. by bike? c. by scooter? d. by car? e. by transit?

☆ Bonus (100 points)

Interview 2 customers leaving the store. Did they come by bike, foot, car, public transport? Why did they choose this store? Take detailed notes.

Information

As bikes are an important part of commuting (and traveling), they also require parking spaces. Amsterdam offers a variety of parking spaces only made for bicycles. There are multiple sections, stands and facilities. Due to the growing number of bicycles, the city wants to prevent them ending up on the streets by offering facilities. Additionally, in a facility your bike will be safe from theft and the weather.

Most of the facilities are free of charge, at least for a starting period. The underground parking facility at the Roeterseilandcampus of the University of Amsterdam offers more than 4000 places for its students. Students start and end their school day at this bike parking facility, where they share and take part in a cycling culture.
